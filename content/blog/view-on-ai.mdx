**TL;DR**

AI has changed how I approach technology and creativity. Before AI, I felt limited because I was not always able to keep up with rapidly changing technologies. Now, AI helps bridge the gap between my current skills and the ideas I want to build.

This reminded me of an old dream I had as a kid: building grand libraries across my country. With AI, I started imagining a new version of that dream—not just providing books, but improving education itself.

The idea is to use locally running AI systems in rural schools as teaching partners. Each school would have a laptop with an AI assistant that helps students ask questions, provides personalized explanations based on their age and environment, and collects learning data to improve education.

The long-term vision is to create a system where talented students from rural areas can be discovered, supported, and given opportunities to continue their education.

I don't know if I will actually build this system, and many technical and organizational challenges remain. But this is a dream I want to explore, and I hope to discuss it further during my graduate studies.

Let's start from there: how do I want to become a teacher?

I don't want to become a teacher for rich kids who always think they deserve everything handed to them. I think that is a bad habit during the learning phase.

Instead, I want to teach children from rural areas who don't have enough opportunities to receive a proper education and whose parents may not be educated either.

Do I know everything? No.  
Do I know how to teach? No.

But one thing I imagine is the smiles on their faces when they learn something new. I can also tell jokes sometimes if that is what makes them happy. Haha.

The plan is to teach them for one month about what I know and what I assume is important. Then, I will move around the country, teaching kids from rural areas each month.

The problem is, whether I can teach them properly or not depends on having a proper system. I cannot do everything alone.

If we can create an AI that can run locally inside a single laptop, and these laptops can still receive updates regularly, that will be the start of the journey.

I will provide laptops for each school in rural areas. For now, let's assume I have 100 laptops for 100 schools.

For each laptop, there will be an operator sitting beside it: the AI. I imagine these operators as knowledgeable teachers.

For those 100 schools, I will create a small organization whose job is to provide study materials, organize data, manage the AI, and handle all those things.

## The Learning System

The lectures will work like this: we will reduce teaching time and add asking time after each lecture.

Every student has to ask a question after the class is finished. The operator will type those questions into the AI for the students.

This stage is an open-topic session. How will the operator ask these questions to the AI?

One thing I have in mind is that we need two layers.

The first layer is translation. The kids will not be speaking English, so we need to understand what they actually want to ask.

The second layer is context. In some rural areas, we do not have enough data about their environment, and we need to consider their age. We cannot explain high school-level concepts to kids who have just started school.

So, what data should we collect?

- Their questions
- Their context
- Their age

## Evaluating Learning Outcomes

To evaluate their learning outcomes, should I make exams? How should I make them?

This is an open-topic session.

My idea is like this: I will make two exams, a mid-term exam and a final exam.

The mid-term exam is where I will make adjustments.

The period before the mid-term will be my domain. During this period, kids will ask questions, and their questions will be collected.

I will train an exam model based on all of those questions and their interests.

Before the mid-term exam, maybe three weeks before the exam, I will release study materials for the mid-term exam.

The materials will include all the topics that kids in the same age group asked about.

Since I reduce teaching time, traditional study materials will also be reduced.

The mid-term exam will be about half and half.

The first half will cover study materials from the beginning of the year. The second half will be based on collective interests from the students.

Why did I say the mid-term exam will be my domain?

My original idea includes not only teaching kids from rural areas but also nurturing them with global-standard education.

I cannot open 100 schools in rural areas, but I can (I wish I could) open one school in the main city with all the equipment and support needed for those kids.

The purpose of the mid-term exam is to select kids who can continue learning.

Let's say there are five subjects. Every kid does not have to become excellent in every subject. Instead, they can focus on their favorite subject.

For each subject, I will give full scholarships to the top students.

Those talented kids will come to the main campus to continue studying and later become young leaders who help their own communities.

Other kids can still continue focusing on their studies for the real final exam, even if they did not get the top scores.

Yeah, now that I have written it out, the school and scholarship system feels like something that is already in place.

So, this idea may or may not be original, but it is something that came to my mind last night.

Will I do it?

Yeah, this is how I upgraded my dream of building grand libraries around the country into something more like applying AI as a teaching partner and helping kids from rural areas by bridging the gap between fortunate and unfortunate children.
