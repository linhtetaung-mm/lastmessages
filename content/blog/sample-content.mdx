Sometimes I feel the internet rewards speed more than care.

There is always another framework, another release, another trend to follow. It's easy to believe that shipping more means creating more value.

I've found the opposite to be true.

## Small steps

Instead of trying to finish everything in a weekend, I like to build one small improvement at a time.

Some days that means fixing a tiny animation.

Other days it means deleting code that no longer serves a purpose.

Both are progress.

## What I'm learning

- Simplicity is usually harder than complexity.
- Good defaults reduce future decisions.
- A project grows healthier when it's easy to maintain.

> "Software ages best when its author enjoys returning to it."
