I always wanted to create a personal website to showcase my projects and things like that.

To be honest, I'm not a typical web developer, and I hate CSS. Saying I hate CSS doesn't mean I enjoy backend development either. I sometimes build small websites, but I really don't want to become a web developer. So you might ask, what do I actually want to become?

When I was a child, I wanted to become a writer. Especially classic Burmese writers like Shwe U Daung, Thaw Thar Swe, and P Moe Hnin. They are some of my favourite writers. I love reading their biographies, and one thing they seem to have in common is that they all had multiple lovers. They weren't particularly wealthy or anything, but their lives were rich and full of interesting stories.

I wanted to become a writer, a philosopher, or maybe a psychologist. Something along those lines.

Nowadays, many great developers have personal websites like this. Some are very minimalistic and contain nothing but blog posts. So here I am. Not to showcase my projects or even my writing, but simply to share my thoughts and notes.

For heaven knows, I might be the only person reading these posts in the future. *Haha.* I'm joking. To be honest, I don't usually go back and read things I've written myself. But who knows? Maybe I'll learn something along the way, and maybe you'll agree with me from time to time.

## Why "last messages"?

For the record, you might wonder why I named this website **last messages**.

One day last month, I was thinking about how AI is trained on human knowledge. I had also watched movies like *Transcendence*, where AI becomes something like a digital replica of a brilliant scientist.

It made me wonder whether, in the future, we might be able to preserve not only someone's knowledge but also their way of thinking.

My first idea was to build a note-taking website where people could record their daily activities, some private and some public. If a user stopped coming online for a certain period of time, the system would assume they had passed away and make all of their notes public.

Maybe that's a strange idea.

But my thinking was simple: while I'm alive, I care about my privacy. After I'm gone... I don't think I would care anymore.

That was the original idea.

## Building this website

This month, while figuring out how to build my personal website, I spent quite a bit of time looking at frameworks like Astro, Hugo, and Jekyll.

Along the way, I discovered **Obsidian**.

It's an amazing application and almost perfect for the kind of note-taking I want to do in the future.

Notion... well, Notion just isn't for me.

I tried using it after getting my MacBook last year, but I never really enjoyed it. Apple's Notes app is nice, but it's also missing a lot of things I personally want.

When I found Obsidian, it honestly felt like meeting a childhood friend again after years apart.

Next month (August 2026), I'll be starting my Master's degree in Data Science and Artificial Intelligence at the Asian Institute of Technology. I have a feeling Obsidian is going to become a big part of that journey.

## So...

Putting all those thoughts together, I decided to call this website **last messages**, even though this is only the beginning of my journey and I still have a long way to go.

After I finished building it with Codex and deployed everything, I searched Google out of curiosity and discovered there's already a service called **Last Messages** that sends messages after you're gone.

I guess someone beat me to the name.

I don't think I'll need that service, though. My repository is public, and anyone can read my notes anyway.

Well... after solving the little puzzle first.

Good luck to you.

And good luck to me too.
